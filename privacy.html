Privacy Policy — Blaze Kick

Last updated: 14 July 2026
Blaze Kick does not collect, store, or share any personal data.
There are no accounts, no servers, no analytics, and no advertising.

What data we collect

None. Blaze Kick does not ask for your name, email address, phone number, location, contacts, photos, or any other personal information. We do not create a user account for you, and we have no server that could receive your data.

What is stored on your device

The game saves your progress locally on your own device, using your browser's standard localStorage. This includes:

  • Which stages you have unlocked and how many stars you earned
  • Your coin balance and the items and skins you own
  • Your settings (sound, vibration, control sensitivity)
  • A display name, if you chose to enter one

This information never leaves your device. It is not uploaded anywhere, and we cannot see it. If you uninstall the game or clear the app's data, it is permanently deleted.

Advertising and analytics

Blaze Kick contains no advertising and no analytics or tracking tools. No third party receives any information about you or about how you play.

Purchases

Blaze Kick contains no in-app purchases. All coins are earned by playing the game. Nothing in the game can be bought with real money.

Permissions

The game may use your device's vibration function to give feedback when the ball is struck. You can turn this off at any time in Settings. No other device permission is requested.

Children

Blaze Kick is suitable for all ages. Because the game collects no personal data whatsoever, it collects no data from children either.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of Blaze Kick introduces advertising, analytics, or purchases, this policy will be updated before that version is released, and the date at the top of this page will change.
